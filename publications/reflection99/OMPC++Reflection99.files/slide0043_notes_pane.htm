This is a comparison  of  OpenJIT and the general JIT.
A general JIT compiler gets a bytecode, and performs the general optimization and then generates native code.
OpenJIT gets the bytecode and metacode, customization which followed the metacode in addition to general optimization is performed, and a native code is generated. In JIT compiling phase, self-application will be also performed.

The conventional JIT is adhock construction, but OpenJIT is based on Reflection and Open Implementation. Most of OpenJIT is written in Java. In the future, all of OpenJIT code will be written in Java.
